Cyber Security

What is a Threat?

A cyber security threat refers to any malicious attack used to unlawfully access data, disrupt operations or damage information. They can come from a range of sources, whether it be an ex-employee, a corporate spy, terrorist group or just a lone hacker.
Attackers can use these attacks to gain access to a wide range of personal data like credit card information or healthcare data. An recent example of a large scale cyber attack is the 2017 Equifax breach, which ended up compromising the data of 143 million consumers.
This data included birth dates, Social Security numbers and addresses. The hackers can then use this data to commit identity fraud.

Types of Threats.

Here is a range of threats that any professional or continual user of computers should know:

  • Malware - This means malicious software and some examples of such are spyware, ransomware, viruses and worms. Once activated, Cisco reports that it can block access to parts of the divice - ransomware -, install additional malware and covertly steal data - spyware.
  • DoS - This means a Denial of service attack and is done through attacks that flood a computer or network so it is unable to fulfill requests.
  • Man in the middle - This type of attack is done through a hacker inserting themselves between a 2 party transaction. After doing this, they can filter and steal the data.
  • Phishing - these attacks use fake communication such as spam emails or fake phone messages to trick the reader into opening it and installing Malware or inputting personal information.
  • Password Attacks - these attacks can be done through either brute force or social engineering to give the hacker access to user's password and through that, their information.
